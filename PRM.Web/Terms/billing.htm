When we use information from your check to make an electronic fund transfer, funds may be withdrawn from your account as soon as the same day we receive your payment, and you will not receive your check back from your institution. You certify you have sufficient funds in the account provided for all electronic debit entries. Electronic debit entries shall be initiated by CareBlue to pay provider service charges and other charges and fees, including, if applicable, any fee charged to finance owed amounts that are not paid in full by the due date, for or associated with the above listed statements or other invoices as authorized and the entries shall constitute your receipt for the transaction(s). No payment to CareBlue shall be deemed to have been made unless and until CareBlue receives actual credit. You also understand that if a correction of any entry is necessary, it may involve an adjustment to your account. You understand your direct electronic payment of the amounts shown on your billing schedule will be debited on or after the due date indicated on that schedule and that you should continue to pay any paper bills you are sent prior to receiving the schedule. You understand that you will thereafter be sent a schedule only after your claim has been adjudicated by your insurance carrier or if your estimated amount changes during the scheduled payment period. CareBlue reserves the right to refuse or terminate electronic payments services. This agreement is to remain in effect until CareBlue terminates it or until you call 1-866-220-2500 to notify CareBlue in writing of termination or, if you are a registered web portal user, you can log on to the careblue.com/pay site and request removal. You understand CareBlue has 5 business days to act on your request. You understand that you have the right to contact your financial institution to place a one-time stop payment.

Billing and Refund Policy

If your insurance pays more than the estimated amount, a refund will be process within 15 days from the date payment is received from your insurance company. Often times, insurance companies will send an Explanation of Benefits to the patient before remitting payment to us. Once the insurance payment is processed by our billing service, a patient refund will be issued. If a refund is in order, it will be processed via the same method as the original payment or, at our discretion, a check may be issued to you. If payment was made by credit card, we will credit your account. While every effort is made to provide refunds in a timely fashion, your financial institution may take several days to reflect refund transactions. Please allow 7-10 days for a credit to appear on your credit card statement after a credit is made. If payment was made by check, the refund check will be mailed within 15 days. If your insurance pays less than the estimated amount, you will receive a statement in the mail or by email if you elected to receive electronic correspondence. Upon notification of an outstanding balance, the total amount is due from the patient. Your cooperation with this is greatly appreciated. Please note, if your insurance company does not remit payment to us after submitting a claim, the patient will be responsible for the entire balance for services rendered. Patients can then contact their insurance carrier to obtain reimbursement directly. Any unpaid balances beyond 30 days will be subject to late fees and finance charges. If you elect to save credit card, debit card or checking methods of payment for future use or as part of an automatic payment or credit plan, CareBlue may occasionally re-verify the validity of those account with your card issuer or financial institution after long periods of inactivity.



Credit Reporting

This is a required legal notice. If you fail to pay your bill, this office at its discretion may submit a negative credit report to a credit reporting agency, which will negatively affect your credit report.



Returned Payments

If your payment is returned for any reason, we will resubmit it electronically. A charge may apply for each payment returned.
